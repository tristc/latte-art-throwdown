## PHASE 1: LATTE ART THROWDOWN SOFTWARE

### Launch: FIRST

**What It Is:**
- Free member accounts
- NO actual member features (no paid benefits)
- ONLY function: Participate in or host latte art throwdowns

**Strategic Purpose:**
- **Prove value creation** — Show the world WEC can build useful tools
- **Open doors** — Creates opportunities through demonstrated capability
- **Low barrier entry** — Gets people into the ecosystem
- **Data collection** — Start building user base and behavioral data
- **Proof of concept** — Test throwdown software mechanics

**What Users Get (Free):**
- Account creation
- Access to latte art throwdown events
- Ability to host their own throwdown
- Basic profile

**What Users DON'T Get (Yet):**
- No membership benefits
- No education content
- No job board
- No reviews
- No CV features

**Key Message:** "We're building tools for the coffee community. Try this."

---

## PHASE 2: ESPRESSO THROWDOWN + MEMBERSHIP PLATFORM

### Launch: SECOND (Cautiously)

**What It Is:**
- Espresso throwdown software (free participation like latte art)
- **OFFICIAL MEMBERSHIP PLATFORM** (paid subscription)
- $5/$25/$50 tiers activated

**Strategic Caution:**
- **SCA is watching** — They could pivot and capture audience if they predict moves
- **Stealth mode** — Don't reveal full roadmap publicly
- **Test quietly** — Soft launch, iterate fast

**What The Membership Platform Includes:**
- All throwdown access (latte art + espresso)
- Early membership benefits TBD
- Foundation for future features

**Why Espresso + Membership Together:**
- Espresso throwdowns prove broader platform capability
- Membership launch converts free users to paid
- Shows growth trajectory
- Revenue activation

**Competitive Risk:**
- SCA has resources to copy features
- Speed matters — capture market first
- Don't telegraph moves in advance

## PHASE 3: CROWDFUNDING CAMPAIGN

### Launch: Around Same Time as Phase 2

**Primary Objectives:**

### 1. Member Acquisition (Especially from SCA)
- **Target:** SCA members frustrated with the organization
- **Message:** "Leave SCA. Join the movement that actually cares."
- **Tactic:** Expose SCA fraud directly

### 2. Education Platform Reveal
- **Disclose:** Full education platform plans
- **Goal:** Recruit talent (instructors, curriculum developers, partners)
- **Message:** "We're building the education system coffee deserves"

### 3. Coffee/Cafe Review Feature Announcement
- **Feature:** All members can give reviews
- **Simple reviews:** Star ratings, quick comments
- **Complex reviews:** Detailed breakdowns, photos, criteria-based
- **Positioning:** "Yelp for coffee, but actually useful"

### 4. CV/Professional Feature (LinkedIn Replacement)
- **Feature:** Coffee-specific professional profiles
- **Verified credentials:** Q-Grader, AST, competition rankings
- **Skills:** Equipment expertise, origin knowledge
- **Message:** "LinkedIn doesn't understand coffee. We do."

### 5. Event Hosting Platform (Eventbrite Replacement)
- **Feature:** Coffee industry event hosting and ticketing
- **Target:** All coffee events currently on Eventbrite
- **Value Prop:** "Eventbrite takes 10%. We take 3%. Your money, when you need it."
- **Key Benefits:**
  - 60%+ lower fees than Eventbrite (3% vs 6.6%+)
  - Instant payouts (vs Eventbrite's 5+ day delay)
  - Coffee-specific features (equipment tracking, origin display, cupping sheets)
  - Real human support (vs Eventbrite's AI chatbot)
  - You own your attendee data
  - Purpose-built for coffee events
- **Event Types Supported:**
  - Cuppings and tastings
  - Latte art classes
  - Barista training
  - Roastery tours
  - Coffee festivals
  - Equipment demonstrations
  - Community meetups
- **Attack Message:** "Stop paying Eventbrite 10% for generic tools that don't understand coffee. Host your events where coffee people actually hang out."

### 6. Crypto/NFT Tease
- **Tactic:** Drop hints only — don't fully reveal
- **Purpose:** Generate buzz, attract crypto-curious investors
- **Positioning:** "The future of coffee is on-chain" (vague, intriguing)

### Campaign Mechanics

**What's Being Sold:**
- Preselling memberships at campaign rates
- Early bird pricing
- Lifetime membership options (limited)
- Founding member status

**Funding Purpose:**
- Final software builds
- Feature development
- Team expansion
- Marketing push

**Success Metrics:**
- Number of members acquired
- SCA defections captured
- Talent recruitment (education team)
- Revenue raised

**What SCA Could Do If They Predict Moves:**

2. **Lower membership prices** — Undercut WEC pricing
3. **Announce education platform** — Beat WEC to market
4. **Partner with crypto/NFT** — Capture that narrative
5. **Improve job board** — Address LinkedIn gap first

**Mitigation Strategies:**
- **Stealth development** — Don't announce until ready to launch
- **Speed to market** — Launch fast, iterate publicly
- **Brand loyalty** — Build community that won't defect
- **Mission differentiation** — SCA can't copy the "fair trade" ethos authentically
- **Content moat** — Documentary, storytelling, personality-driven content

**What NOT To Reveal Early:**
- Full feature roadmap
- Crypto/NFT specifics
- Education platform details
- B League timing
- Partnership negotiations
