# WEC Brand Guidelines — Master Reference

## Brand Identity

### Overview
World Espresso Championship (WEC) is a premier high-stakes competition founded in 2022. The brand represents excellence, precision, and the elevation of barista craftsmanship through standardized competition formats.

### Brand Positioning
**"To seek the best coffee for the benefit of all."**

At World Espresso Championships we believe that coffee is much more than just a drink - it's a force for good in the world and in the lives of everyone it touches.

Every bean has a story, and every cup has the potential to create real impact.

We believe our purpose and role in society is to bring together farmers, roasters, baristas and coffee lovers, to seek out and develop the best coffee, whatever it takes, while ensuring every person in the value chain is recognised and rewarded and given access to the right opportunities in life.

Through shared experiences and data-driven insights we bridge the gap between bean and cup, raising industry standards and enriching lives. Because the best coffee is about more than just taste - it's about the people behind it, fairness and sustainable quality.

## The Philosophy of Subjectivity

### The Fundamental Truth
**On planet Earth, humans have free will — the freedom to like or dislike anything they want.**

This is not a bug. This is the defining feature of human experience.

Coffee quality is subjective because **people are sovereign over their own preferences**. No certification, no score sheet, no expert opinion can override an individual's right to enjoy what they enjoy.

### Why This Matters
The coffee industry has spent decades trying to convince people that they "don't know good coffee." That their preferences are wrong. That they need to be educated into liking what experts deem worthy.

**We reject this completely.**

Quality is subjective. Taste is personal. And that is exactly as it should be.

### The WEC Position
- **There are no wrong answers.** Your taste is valid.
- **If you like it, it's good coffee.** Full stop.
- **The question isn't "Is this good?"** — it's "Can the next coffee be better?"
- **Empowerment over education.** We don't teach you what to like. We help you discover what YOU like.

### Contrast with SCA/WBC
| SCA/WBC Approach | WEC Approach |
|------------------|--------------|
| "We'll teach you what's good" | "You already know what's good for you" |
| Scoresheets define quality | Your palate defines quality |
| Experts determine worth | Individuals determine worth |
| Objective standards | Subjective truth |
| Education into conformity | Empowerment of preference |

### The Ethical Foundation
Denying someone's taste preferences is denying their autonomy. It's saying: *"Your experience of the world is invalid. Our expertise supersedes your reality."*

We believe the opposite: **Your experience of coffee is the only one that matters to you.**

Our role is not to override your preferences. It's to:
- Help you discover them
- Help you communicate them  
- Help you find coffees that match them
- Help you understand WHY you like what you like

### The Scientific Validation
Comparison-based judging (A vs B) acknowledges this truth. It doesn't ask judges to agree on "the best." It asks individuals: *"Which do YOU prefer?"*

The aggregate of individual preferences reveals quality more honestly than any imposed standard.

## Competition Context

### Brand Positioning
- **Differentiation**: Unlike WBC (World Barista Championship) where competitors bring their own exotic beans and create signature drinks, WEC standardizes everything — same coffee, machine, grinder, water. The barista's extraction technique is the only variable.
- **Relationship to WBC**: Complementary format, not competitive
- **Prestige**: Elite entry (national champions only)
