## Core Strategic Frameworks Established

### 1. The Ecosystem Architecture
| Brand | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| OCC (Objective Coffee Community) | Central membership platform - "governance body of coffee world" |
| WCT (World Coffee Tournaments) | Umbrella for all competition brands |
| WEC | Espresso competitions |
| WLT | Latte-art tournaments (online) |
| WCT | Café tournaments |
| WICC | Instant coffee championships |
| WBN | World Barista Network |
| SCE | Specialty Coffee Education |

### 2. Mission Statement
"To become THE governance body of the coffee world - through centralising information, optimising coffee (in the cup), and creating opportunity – while ensuring the majority of the value is given to the people that drive the industry."

### 3. Core Differentiation from SCA/WBC

**WBC Philosophy:**
- Competitors bring own beans (often exotic/expensive)
- Signature drinks (elaborate creative beverages)
- 15-minute performance scoring
- "Deliberation" (score manipulation backstage)
- Judges compare to memory

**WEC Philosophy:**
- Standardized equipment (same coffee, machine, grinder, water)
- Barista is ONLY variable
- Comparison-based judging (A vs B)
- No scoresheets, no deliberation possible
- Head-to-head elimination

### 4. The Consumer Sensory Protocol
**One Objective Factor:** Smoothness (texture)
**Four Subjective Factors:** Sweetness, Acidity, Flavor, Overall Preference

**The WEC Message:**
"There are no wrong answers. Your taste is valid. If you like it, it's good coffee. But, can the next coffee be better?"

### 5. Business Model - Tripartite Value Distribution
| Stakeholder | Traditional Model | WEC Model |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Farmers | 5-10% | 25-35% |
| Baristas | 0-5% | 15-25% |
| Cafes | 20-30% | 25-35% |
| Big Roaster | 40-60% | 0-5% (disintermediated) |
| Platform | 0% | 10-15% |

### 6. The Judging System
- Comparison-based (LEFT/RIGHT) - never subjective scoring
- Point allocation:
  - Latte Art: 3 points
  - Taste Balance: 1 point
  - Tactile: 1 point
  - Flavour: 1 point
  - Overall Preference: 5 points
  - Total: 11 points per judge - all binary choices

### 8. The "Deliberation" Scandal
WBC practice of backstage score manipulation:
- Judges forced to "recalibrate" scores
- Social pressure eliminates variance
- Geographic bias (winners chosen based on future host locations)
- **WEC immunity:** No scores to manipulate, binary preference only

### 9. Education Fraud Documentation
**SCA Claim:** "Globally recognized standard" courses
**AST Reality:** No courses exist—ASTs create 100% of content
- 1,800+ ASTs each teaching different material
- Students pay $800-1,200 for testing service disguised as education
- SCA extracts €1,500+ license fees, provides zero content

### 10. Latte Art Throwdown Platform ("SmackDown Central")
**Core concept:** Infrastructure for global throwdowns
**Key features:**
- Digital bracket management
- Photo-based judging
- Global rankings
- Member conversion funnel to WEC

### 11. Origin Story (From Founder)
"I was asked to create a competition that promoted Dalla Corte's latest model. Rather than hosting another latte art throwdown, I decided to create a comp that was missing from the market. Since WBC does not have a compulsory round, nothing existed that proved objectively which barista made the best coffee. We coined it the World Espresso Championship."

### 12. Founder Background
"Over 25 years in the industry, from washing dishes to owning cafes, from barista to competitor and winner, from student to teacher (founded multiple Academies), from roaster to roasting brand founder, from store owner to franchise operations manager. 25 years studying the industry revealed that coffee is still very much a slave trade. I feel compelled to do something about it."

### 13. SCA CVA Research Findings

- **WEC counter-position:** Collect data from consumers about the FINISHED CUP
- Green coffee is half-finished product—end cup is the only product that matters

### 15. Scientific Backing Verified
**Comparison vs. Absolute Judging:**
- "Humans have difficulty judging absolute values but are pretty effective at perceiving and comparing relative differences" (Stone et al. 2021)
- Thurstone's Law of Comparative Judgement (1927)
- Paired comparison tests are ISO-standardized (ISO 5495:2005)

**Smoothness as Objective Quality:**
- University of São Paulo & Oxford study (2019-2020)
- Smooth cups = perceived sweeter
- Rough cups = perceived more acidic AND dry
- Universally negative: astringency, drying sensation
