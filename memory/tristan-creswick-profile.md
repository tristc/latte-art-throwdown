## The Philosophy

### On Education
> "His experience has caused him to recognize the results that education can bring so he continues to be a scholar himself and has made a commitment to educating others."

> "We have been designing courses and training and assessing coffee skills long before the Specialty Coffee Association (SCA) was even operating."

### On Quality (The Critical Insight)
> "Quality is subjective, a personal preference, and as an industry professional we need to be able to embrace and teach that (even though it is very difficult to teach)."

**The Philosophical Foundation:**
On planet Earth, humans have free will — the freedom to like or dislike anything they want. This is the fundamental reason why coffee quality is subjective. No expert, no certification, no authority can override an individual's right to their own preferences.

**Tristan's Position:**
- Subjectivity is not a flaw to be corrected — it's a human right to be honored
- The industry has spent decades trying to "educate" people into liking what experts deem worthy
- This approach denies people's autonomy and invalidates their experience
- **"There are no wrong answers. Your taste is valid."**
- **"If you like it, it's good coffee."**

### On Competition
> "Most barista competitions are about who can serve the judges coffee that they prefer on that specific day. It's not really an honest competition if the judges have to compare apples and oranges."

> "While judges can objectively assess who wins a long jump or shot put championship, judges must agree who makes the 'best' coffee in a coffee competition – a purely subjective decision."

**The WEC Solution:**
Rather than forcing agreement on "the best," WEC asks individuals: **"Which do YOU prefer?"**

The aggregate of individual preferences reveals quality more honestly than any imposed standard. Comparison-based judging (A vs B) honors subjectivity rather than trying to eliminate it.

### On WEC's Purpose
> "Firstly, we're hoping to create an innovative space that pushes the limits of espresso recipes by giving competitors a lot more flexibility with their brewing recipes, as well as having highly experienced judges taking part, too."

> "Secondly, we're hoping the competitions have a fun atmosphere that fosters progression and creativity."

### On Coffee
> "Coffee has given me so many great experiences, introduced me to some of the most amazing people, and allowed me to travel all over the world. I look forward to showing you how you can have the same exciting opportunities."
