## The Judging System: Comparison-Based, Not Scored

### The Problem with Traditional Competitions

The World Barista Championship (WBC) uses subjective scoring and "deliberation" — a process where judges are pressured backstage to change scores until they reach "consensus." This:
- Eliminates true variance in preference
- Introduces geographic bias (winners chosen based on future host locations)
- Invalidates individual taste

### The WEC Solution

**Comparison-Based Judging (A vs B):**
- No scoresheets
- No deliberation possible
- Binary preference only: "Which do YOU prefer?"

**The Protocol:**
1. Two competitors extract espresso simultaneously
2. Judges taste blindly
3. On the count of three, judges point to their preference
4. Winner advances — no discussion, no manipulation

**The Science:**
- Thurstone's Law of Comparative Judgement (1927)
- ISO 5495:2005 — paired comparison is the international standard
- Reliability scores: .96-.98 (vs. .70-.80 for traditional marking)

### The Sensory Framework

| Factor | Type | Weight |
|--------|------|--------|
| Latte Art | Visual | 3 points |
| Taste Balance | Subjective | 1 point |
| Tactile (Smoothness) | Objective | 1 point |
| Flavour | Subjective | 1 point |
| Overall Preference | Subjective | 5 points |
| **Total** | | **11 points** |

**Note:** Smoothness is the only objective factor — and it's where BWT's water filtration makes the measurable difference.

## Appendix: The Science of Comparison-Based Judging

### Why A vs B Beats Scoresheets

**The Research:**

> "Humans have difficulty judging absolute values but are pretty effective at perceiving and comparing relative differences."  
> — Stone et al. (2021), Journal of Sensory Studies

**Thurstone's Law of Comparative Judgement (1927):**
The probability that a judge will prefer option A over option B is a function of the difference in their perceived quality. This is mathematically more reliable than absolute scoring.

**ISO 5495:2005:**
Paired comparison tests are the international standard for sensory analysis. WEC's judging protocol follows this standard precisely.

**Reliability Scores:**
- Traditional scoring: .70-.80 reliability
- Paired comparison: .96-.98 reliability

### Why Water Matters

**The São Paulo/Oxford Study (2019-2020):**
- Smooth cups = perceived sweeter
- Rough cups = perceived more acidic AND dry
- Astringency universally negative

**The Conclusion:**
Water quality directly impacts the one objective factor in WEC judging: **smoothness (tactile)**. BWT filtration doesn't just clean water — it enables the fair expression of barista skill.
