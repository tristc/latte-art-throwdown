## Tier-by-Tier Consumer Analysis

### TIER 1: $5/Month — The Mass Consumer Tier

**Target:** Every coffee drinker who cares about quality, fairness, or competition

**Addressable Market:**
- 400M–600M specialty coffee consumers globally
- Target: 0.01%–0.025% conversion = 40,000–150,000 subscribers

**Why $5 Works:**
- Below the cognitive barrier (cheaper than a single latte)
- Matches successful niche platforms (Chess.com entry tier, Patreon median)
- Impulse-buy territory — "less than my Netflix ad tier"

**Value Proposition for Consumers:**
- Vote on competition outcomes (democratizing coffee judging)
- Exclusive behind-the-scenes content
- Early access to event streams
- Support the "fair trade revolution" (farmers getting paid)
- Community belonging ("I'm part of the movement")

**Benchmarks:**
- Chess.com: 1.5M paying from 200M users = 0.75% conversion
- Patreon: Top creators see 2–10% follower-to-patron conversion
- **Conservative WEC estimate:** 0.01% of 500M = 50,000 subscribers
- **Optimistic WEC estimate:** 0.03% of 500M = 150,000 subscribers

**Revenue:**
- Conservative: 50,000 × $5 = $250,000/month = **$3M/year**
- Optimistic: 150,000 × $5 = $750,000/month = **$9M/year**

### TIER 2: $25/Month — The Enthusiast/Superfan Tier

**Target:** Hardcore coffee enthusiasts, home baristas, aspiring competitors, "coffee nerds"

**Conversion from Tier 1:**
- Industry benchmark: 5–15% of base tier upgrades to premium
- Conservative: 10% of $5 tier upgrades = 5,000–15,000 subscribers

**Why $25 Works:**
- Comparable to gym memberships, premium streaming bundles
- Positioned as "serious enthusiast" not "casual consumer"
- 5× the base tier = significant revenue multiplier with fewer subscribers

**Value Proposition:**
- Everything in $5 tier PLUS:
- Monthly virtual cupping sessions (mailed samples)
- Direct messaging with WEC competitors
- Exclusive Discord channels
- Annual swag box
- Early ticket access to live events
- Members-only competitions

**Benchmarks:**
- Patreon $25 tiers: Typically 10–20% of total patron base
- DAZN sports streaming: $20M subscribers at $15–25/month
- **Conservative WEC estimate:** 5,000 subscribers
- **Optimistic WEC estimate:** 15,000 subscribers

**Revenue:**
- Conservative: 5,000 × $25 = $125,000/month = **$1.5M/year**
- Optimistic: 15,000 × $25 = $375,000/month = **$4.5M/year**

---

### TIER 3: $50/Month — The Champion/Advocate Tier

**Target:** Ultra-supporters, small café owners, coffee influencers, industry allies, "true believers"

**Conversion from Lower Tiers:**
- Industry benchmark: 1–3% of paying user base
- Conservative: 2% of total base = 1,000–3,000 subscribers

**Why $50 Works:**
- Positions as exclusive club / inner circle
- Comparable to premium fitness memberships, wine clubs
- Aligns with "support the mission" positioning (social good component)

**Value Proposition:**
- Everything in previous tiers PLUS:
- Quarterly premium coffee shipments (retail value >$50)
- Name/logo in event credits
- Annual virtual meetup with WEC leadership
- Input on competition format decisions
- VIP hospitality at live events
- Direct line to founder team

**Benchmarks:**
- High-end Patreon tiers: 1–5% of total at $50–$100
- Wine club memberships: $50–$100/month common
- **Conservative WEC estimate:** 1,000 subscribers
- **Optimistic WEC estimate:** 3,000 subscribers

**Revenue:**
- Conservative: 1,000 × $50 = $50,000/month = **$600K/year**
- Optimistic: 3,000 × $50 = $150,000/month = **$1.8M/year**

## Critical Success Factors for Consumer Adoption

### 1. The Mission Must Be Crystal Clear
"Your $5/month directly supports coffee farmers and baristas — not middlemen"
- Transparency dashboard showing exactly where money goes
- Monthly impact reports ("This month, members funded $50K in competitor prizes")

### 2. Content Must Be Entertainment, Not Education
- **Not:** "How to dial in your espresso" tutorials
- **Yes:** Competitor drama, comeback stories, underdog narratives
- Documentary-style storytelling over instructional content
- Behind-the-scenes access that feels exclusive

### 3. Community Ownership
- Members vote on competition formats
- Members choose "fan favorite" awards
- Regional representatives/chapters
- Members feel like stakeholders, not customers

### 4. The $5 Price Anchoring
- Position against Netflix ($7–$17): "Cheaper than streaming, more impactful"
- Position against a latte: "Skip one coffee per month, change the industry"
- Annual discount: $50/year (17% savings) to improve cash flow

### 5. Referral Flywheel
- Every member gets a referral link
- Rewards stack (1 referral = 1 month free, 5 referrals = annual upgrade)
- Gamification of "spreading the mission"
