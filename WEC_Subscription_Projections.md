## Tier-by-Tier Analysis

### TIER 1: $5/Month — Consumer Enthusiast

**Target Audience:**
- Home coffee enthusiasts
- WEC fans who want to support the mission
- Casual viewers of competition content
- Coffee curious consumers wanting education

**Market Size Reference:**
- Global chess enthusiasts: ~600M
- Chess.com paying members: 1.5M (0.75% of 200M users)
- Estimated specialty coffee enthusiasts: 50-75M

**Conservative Estimate (15,000 subscribers):**
- Assumes 0.02-0.03% conversion of global specialty coffee enthusiasts
- Comparable to CuriosityStream's entry-level traction
- 3x SCA's current paid membership (realistic given consumer focus)

**Optimistic Estimate (25,000 subscribers):**
- Strong viral growth via TikTok/YouTube content
- "Queen's Gambit effect" equivalent for coffee
- Active community engagement driving referrals

**Revenue:**
- Conservative: $75,000/month = $900,000/year
- Optimistic: $125,000/month = $1,500,000/year

---

### TIER 2: $25/Month — Professional/Barista

**Target Audience:**
- Working baristas
- Coffee shop owners
- Roasters
- Coffee trainers/educators
- Serious hobbyists with disposable income

**Market Size Reference:**
- Global baristas: ~500,000-750,000 (estimated)
- Coffee shop owners/operators: ~150,000-200,000
- SCA professional membership base: ~8,000 (underperforming due to pricing/value)

**Conservative Estimate (3,000 subscribers):**
- 0.4-0.6% of global professional coffee workforce
- Addresses clear gap: SCA membership at $100/year has ~8,000 members
- WEC at $300/year provides direct value (content, community, competition access)
- Comparable to DAZN's niche sports appeal

**Optimistic Estimate (6,000 subscribers):**
- Captures frustrated SCA members seeking alternative
- Compelling value proposition: competitions, education, community
- Strong word-of-mouth among café networks

**Revenue:**
- Conservative: $75,000/month = $900,000/year
- Optimistic: $150,000/month = $1,800,000/year

**Target Audience:**

**Revenue:**

## Critical Success Factors

To achieve these projections, WEC must:

1. **Content Strategy:**
   - Regular competition livestreams (minimum quarterly major events)
   - Behind-the-scenes competitor stories
   - Educational content (brewing guides, technique breakdowns)
   - Documentary-style storytelling (like Drive to Survive for F1)

2. **Community Building:**
   - Active Discord/community platform
   - Member-only forums and voting rights
   - Local chapter/meetup support
   - Direct access to WEC athletes

3. **Value Differentiation:**
   - Clear contrast with SCA (no deliberation, transparent judging)
   - Exclusive merchandise and early access
   - Voting rights on competition formats/rules
   - Direct financial support to competitors (transparency)

4. **Marketing:**
   - TikTok/YouTube Shorts strategy (coffee content performs well)
   - Partnership with coffee equipment brands
   - Influencer collaborations (coffee YouTubers, barista champions)
   - PR around the "fair trade" mission (farmers getting paid)
